如何应对insults

继续阅读斯多葛学派的思想，今天的收获是如何应对侮辱insults.

大部分人认为最好的回应是反侮辱counterinsult. 斯多葛学派拒绝这种建议。他们给出了他们的advices：

方法一：response with humor. by laughing off an insult, we are *** implying that we do not take the insulter and his insults seriously***.

方法二：No response at all. a nonresponse to an insult makes it look as if we re indifferent to the existence of the insulter, not only we do not take him seriously, but do not take him at all.

方法三：There are times when it is appropriate for us to response vigorously(有力地) to an insult. In this case, we need to keep in mind that we are punishing the insulter not because she has wronged him but to correct her improper behavior. like training an animal.

为什么我们要自找不适

正当对Stoic斯多葛学派有些好奇的时候，就读到一本关于它的书。

Stoic学派有很多有趣的思想和观点，今天读到它的一个观点是：Welcome a degree of discomfort in our life. 今天的人大多都不理解，有舒适的生活不选，为什么要选择让自己不适。Stoic 给出了自愿选择不适带来的三点好处。

  1. Voluntary discomfort can be thought of as a kind of vaccine: by exposing ourselves to a small amount of a weakened virus now, we create in ourselves an immunity that will protect us from a debilitating illness in the future.
  2. A person who periodically experiences minor discomforts will grow confident that he can withstand major discomforts as well.
  3. Undertaking acts of voluntary discomfort helps us appreciate what we already have.

习惯的力量

Naval说：一切皆习惯。 habits are everything - everything we are. we are trained in habits from when we are children. these things become habits - behaviors we learn and integrate into ourselves. when we are older, we are a collection of thousands of habits constantly running subconsciously. You become your habits.

Derek Sivers说：You are defined by what you do repeatedly. your habits create your character . 所以他接着说： Once you decide what is important to you, you know how your ideal self will act and what your ideal day will be. so why not act that way and live that day every day? Commit to your habits to make them rituals. If it is not important, never do it. if it is important, do it every day.

Rockets use most of their fuel in the first minute of flight to escape the pull of gravity. Once they get outside that pull, it is effortless. Same with your habits. Starting is hard. The rest is easy.

看来我需要好好地审视一下自己的习惯了。

Book#03: Show your work

Book#03: Show your work 作者：Kleon, Austin

这是一本很好读的小书，话题最接近我们的生活，Austin教我们如何通过社交媒体来展现我们自己，然后让机会自己找上门来。按规矩，挑几点记录下。

3个受益点：

  1. 关于可以晒什么

    原则：晒自己感兴趣的，同时对别人有用的东西，比如

    • Share your reading list
    • Point to helpful reference materials
    • Create some tutorials and post them on-line. Use pictures, words and video. take people step by step through part of your process.

  2. 读讣告

    他说：Try it: Start reading the obituaries every morning. Obituaries are like near-death experiences for cowards. Thinking about death every morning makes me want to live.

  3. 关于feedback

    我们只应该关注那些在乎我们和我们所作的工作的人的反馈。而不是所有人。

    You want feedback from people who care about you and what you do.

1个实践项：

build a good domain name, own your own space on-line.

Austin苦口婆心地说，If you get one thing out of this book make it this [如果你仅从这本书学到一点，那就做这个]: Go register a domain name去注册一个域名，购买www.[insert your name here].com

恩，这件事需要提上日程了，今年搞定它。
