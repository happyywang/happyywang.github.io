别人的成长之路

今天读到一份Newsletter，写作者讲述自己的成长之路，如何从一个高学学渣，退级报考中专，到毕业后一步一步成长为一位软件系统架构师的成长之路。

他提到一个比喻甚是有触动，他说：我们前进的速度既可以像乌龟一样，也可以像火箭一样，人是一个变量，在乌龟与火箭的状态之中游离，当我们奔跑的动力足够强时，足以附生在火箭上。

他接着说，一个人一旦开始努力时，就不要懊恼过去的乌龟状态，self-pity是一个陷阱，不要落入这种状态，选择停在那里或时驶入一条新的道路，决定权在我们自己手上。

最后他警示读者 不要做大多数。不要听大多数云云，这个很难，那个很难。不管我们现在的学习或者编程水平如何，只要肯下苦功夫，一年时间足以产生质的飞跃。他说在互联网时代，有足够多的自学资源，通过自学便可以改变一个人一生的轨迹。作者走的正是这条路，有感而发吧。

如何实现享受生活又高效储蓄两不误

听了最新一期的三五环播客，挺不错，于是找来嘉宾的书 工薪族财务自由说明书 翻一翻，略有收获。

比如作者聊如何享受生活又高效储蓄两不误。

  1. 砍掉无效消费

  2. 规划预算和建立愿望清单

    有预算会促使我们优先考虑最需要，最重要的东西，而不是满足即时快感。只有当资源有限时，我们才会认真考虑到底什么是必需的。

    愿望清单的两个核心原则：

    • 想要消费，先放入愿望清单
    • 优先实现最高优先级的愿望

消费建议：在令我们怦然心动的需求上不妥协，一步到位，在可有可无的消费上下狠手，甚至直接砍掉。结果将是：伪需求越来越少，真正的需求得到很好的满足，并顺利攒下钱。而不是为了攒钱，拼命压抑全部生活需求。

总之，节流储蓄要建立在可持续的基础上，把有限的预算集中在带来幸福感最强的事物上，砍掉无效消费，把它们转换为财富。

如何增加你的收入

如何增加你的收入，Nick Maggiulli的书Just keep buying列出5点方法

  • Sell your Time/Expertise (Easy to do, low startup cost, but time is limited, does not scale)

  • Sell a skill/Service (higher pay, able to build a brand, but need to invest time to develop marketable skill/service. do not scale easily)

  • Teach People (Easily scalable, but lots of competition. Attracting students can be an ongoing battle)

  • Sell a product (Scalable. but lot of upfront investment and constant marketing)

  • Climb the corporate ladder (Gain skills and experience, less risk around income growth. but do do not control your time and what you do)

    但是作者认为The end goal should be ownership - using your additional income to acquire more income-producing assets.

深度工作系统

Cal Newport给出的深度工作定义是：Professional, activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit. (专注的，不间断，不分心的工作，这种工作会将你的认知能力推到极限)

在信息经济时代，深度工作可以帮助你很好地完成两件事

  • 学习并掌握新的技能
  • 运用这些技能增加你的产出

执行起来的困难因素：

  • 开放式办公环境

  • 即使通信工具，导致频繁的上下文切换

  • 社交媒体身为时间黑洞

关于如何开始深度工作的几点建议

  • 安排时间 （为每天分配深度工作的时间:无meeting, 不和人闲聊，不刷社交网络，关wifi等，把深度工作的时间变成Routine）
  • 创建深度工作环境（环境会成为一个习惯的trigger）
  • 充分利用好深度工作时间(番茄钟法，并focus在最重要的事情上， plan,note,review)

深度工作的四种哲学深度

深度工作的四种哲学深度

  1. The Monastic Philosophy of deep work scheduling(禁欲主义哲学)

    This philosophy attempts to maximize deep effects by eliminating or radically minimizing shallow obligations. Practitioners of the monastic philosophy tend to have a well-defined and highly valued professional goal that they are pursuing and the bulk of their professional success comes from doing this one thing exceptionally well.

  2. The Bimodal Philosophy of deep work scheduling（双峰哲学）

    This philosophy asks that you divide your time, dedicating some clearly defined stretches to deep pursuits and leaving the rest open to everything else. During the deep time, the bimodal worker will act monastically, seeking intense and uninterrupted concentration. during the shallow time, such focus is not prioritized. This devision of time between deep and open can happen on multiple scales. eg: on the scale of a week, you might dedicate a 4-day weekend to depth and the rest to open, or on the scale of a year, you might dedicate one season to contain most of your deep stretches.

  3. The Rhythmic philosophy of deep work scheduling （节奏哲学）

    This philosophy argues that the easiest way to consistently start deep work sessions is to transform them into a simple regular habit. The goal is to generate a rhythm for this work that removes the need for you to invest energy in deciding if and when you are going to go deep.

  4. The journalistic philosophy of deep work scheduling (新闻记者哲学)

    This philosophy needs a ability to rapidly switch your mind from shallow to deep mode which does not come naturally. This habit requires a sense of confidence in your abilities and is typically built on a foundation of existing professional accomplishment. It is not for the deep work novice.

对于创造性工作的一个观察

对于创造性工作的一个观察：

Great creative work is not possible if you are trying to piece together 30 minutes here and 45 minutes there. Large, uninterrupted blocks of time - 3 to 5 hours minimum - create the space needed to find and connect the dots. And one block per week is not enough. There has to be enough slack in the system for multi-day, CPU-intensive synthesis.

Paul Graham称它为Maker’s schedule, 与之对应的是Manager’s schedule: each day is cut into one hour intervals, by default, the people change what they are doing every hour.

显然程序员，作家等属于Maker’s schedule, 把控好自己的时间吧。

The benefits of thinking 10x vs. 10%

The benefits of thinking 10x vs. 10%

first of all, when you are going 10% bigger, you are completing against everybody. Everybody is trying to go 10% bigger. when you are trying to go 10 times bigger, you are there by yourself.

The second thing is, when you are trying to go 10 times bigger, you have to start with a clean sheet of paper, and you approach the problem completely differently.

The third thing is when you try to go 10 times bigger vs. 10% bigger, it is typically not 100 times harder, but the reward is 100 times more.
