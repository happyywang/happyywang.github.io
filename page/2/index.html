David Perell 50天写作课程（一）：为自己而写

订阅了David Perell的50天写作课程，一天一封关于写作技巧的指导邮件，昨天收到第一封，记录一下。

第一天：为自己而写（Write for yourself）

主播Tyler Cowen在他的每期播客中都这样开场：This is the conversation I want to have, not the one you want to have. 听众如果感到失望，他不会在意。因为He wants to have wild and wacky conversations that reflect his interests. by ignoring the wisdom of crowds, he attracted an ocean of like-minded super fans.

Depending on the article, you can either write for yourself or one other person. Thinking about thousands of people at once will drive you crazy because you will not know what to focus on. Do not let a stadium of perspectives paralyze you. Pick one obsessive person to write for.

简言之：为自己而写，写自己感兴趣的话题，最终自然会吸引到志同道合的读者。

断更一次会怎样

日更这个blog超一个月了，一个问题自然就冒出来了：断更一次会怎样? 为此还去搜索了一番，最后在James Clear那里找到了满意的答案。

Every habit streak ends at some point. Perfection is not possible. Before long, an emergency will pop up - you get sick or you have to travel for work or your family needs a little of your time. Whatever this happens to me, I try to remind myself of a simple rule:

Never miss twice.

Generally speaking, the first mistake is never the one that ruins you. It is the spiral of repeated mistakes that follows.

Missing once is an accident, Missing twice is the start of a new habit.

You do not realize how valuable it is to just show up on your bad (or busy) days. Lost days hurt you more than successful days help you.

Noah Kagan的故事

看到推特上有人提到Noah Kagan这个人，thread了8点他的行事风格对他的巨大影响。

  1. Prioritize NOW, not how

    他说：Noah’s instince is to act first, then think later based on feedback. 他则相反：He wanted to over-research stuff first. 但显然：actually doing the thing first gets real-time feedback which is way better than research.

  2. Test then invest

  3. Have ONE clear goal

    Noah learned this from Zucks during his Facebook days. This makes decision-making Way easier.

  4. Default to NO

    with main goal in mind, say NO to things that did not help to get there.

    He said he realized that the real magic of producitivity is to just focus on the few critical drivers and ignore everything else.

  5. What is working & how can we do more of it

  6. Feedback is gift.

  7. Face discomfort

  8. Make it fun.

好奇之下Google了一下Noah Kagan，原来他是Facebook特别早期的员工（#30号），05年加入facebook，当年24岁, 9个月后却被公司开除。为此他损失了1，85亿美元的FB股票，于是他写了一本书，名叫 How I lost 170 Million Dollars: My Time as #30 at Facebook. 书中解释了他被解雇的原因以及他如何在Raddit的”Ask my anything”的聊天中重新成为一名成功企业家的。

尽管他错过了ＦＢ的财富，但此后他创立了自己的２家成功的公司，Sumo和AppSumo, 每家也都有上Million的价值。此外他还主持一个名为”Noah Kagan Presents”的商业播客和拥有一个YouTube频道。可谓是：what does not kill us makes us stronger.

关于如何度过艰难时光，他说：What helped was keeping busy , surrounding myself with positive people and reflecting on what I can learn from the situation.

他对企业家的建议是：Persist. Things take time so do not give up for a month, Impressive things take years. 谈到创业的最佳时机，他建议创业者最好立即开始：Money is everywhere. Go make something so people are EXCITED to give it to you.

  5. 使用键盘快捷键来导航视频 Navigate YouTube with keyboard shortcuts

    常用快捷键：

    k: Pause/Play in player

    m: Mute/unmute the video

    j: seek backward 10 seconds in player

    Number 0 on the seek bar: seek to the beginning of the video

    f: active full screen or exit to full screen

    c: activate subtitles or hide again

    Shift + P: move to the previous video.

每周断网一天会怎样

24/6: 每周断网一天会怎样, 这是一本书的中文译名。英文书名：24/6: The power of unplugging one day a week. 读到书名的第一眼就立刻被吸引。

迅速去豆瓣上了解了一下。据悉，在书中，作者Tiffany Shlain介绍了一种生活在27/7世界中的策略：每周24小时关闭所有屏幕。她和她的家人在过去的近十年里每周践行着这种方法，他们称这种方法为技术安息日（Technology Shabbat)，结果发现他们的生活发生了彻底的改变，他们有了更多时间，更高效率，更好的关系以及更多参与。

听起来挺诱人，但仔细想了想，当下在自己身上实在难以践行：不能听播客，不能看书（书都在Kindle里），不能记步，不能读Newsletter，不能更新Blog…

不过我还是愿意读读这本书，看能不能带给我点其它什么灵感或启发。书已经下载到了kindle，周末读起来。

排队等候时隐藏的快乐

读到一篇旧文The hidden joy of waiting in line，里面描述当下的人们对等待的耐心度精准极了：we want it all and we want it now. 网络用户更是一个没有耐心的群体，他们对视频加载的平均忍耐时间仅2秒钟。移动设备的出现缓解了人们的等待焦虑症，于是出现了一种现象：一有等待时间，人们就会摸出手机，忙着发消息，写邮件，刷社交媒体等等，让自己的大脑一刻不停地被占用。

作者觉得排队等待的这个Idle时间是多么奢侈，为什么不能利用这个时间做点让自己放松的事情呢，而不是把它都花在手机屏幕上。他给出了9个可以在这个时间采取的实际行动：

  1. smile at a stranger
  2. practice a “sights and sounds” meditation.
  3. let someone who is in a rush cut in front of you.
  4. make a mental list of things you are grateful for.
  5. take some deep breaths
  6. daydream
  7. slightly repeat a mantra for inner piece
  8. send a kind thought to someone you love
  9. read a book.

每一点都挺实用，下次超市排队时我会尝试 1 和 3，诊所排队时尝试 9， 煮饭等待时尝试 4 和 5。
