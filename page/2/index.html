我希望在20岁时能学到的15个财务教训

15 financial lessons I wish I would learned at 20 (@Kurtis Hanni)

  • Keep a budget (what you do not track, you do not control, so much money disappeared on stupid things you will never remember)
  • Invest yourself (you are a gem(宝石), if you do not inverst in you, no one will either. Investments made in your 20s will lay the foundation for future growth)
  • Live with less now (quit buying all that junk and live like you have nothing)
  • Just set it and forget it (start investing in index funds today and never look at your account)
  • The marketers know you(There are people with years of experience who know how to speak to you. quit falling prey to their games, Pause before you buy)
  • You have nothing to lose (Failure at 20 leaves a lot of time to recover. You will never be freer than you are today. Take advantage, take risks and try something crazy)
  • Savings gives you options (Saving money is a habit, the sooner you start, the easier it is to continue, set aside 3-6 months for an emergency fund, the peace of mind will free you up and give you options)
  • Compound interest is magic
  • Income solves a lot of problems
  • Experience is extremely important
  • Today is the best time to start a business (The older you get, the more excuses you will have, if you have an idea, chase)
  • **Spend money on experiences, not things **(Things are fleeting, experiences are forever)
  • Multiple streams of income gives you options (start some side hustles and explore your passions, streams of income open up your world to a lot of possibilities)
  • Who you marry with can make or break your finances
  • Your career and outcomes are your responsibility (Take control of your life and take action. No one is going to do it for you)

改变生活的7个认知

改变生活的7个认知，同样来自Mark Manson

  • Nobody is paying attention to you as much as you are to yourself. That thing that mortifies you? Other people probably do not even notice
  • People mistake comfort for happiness. They are completely different things
  • No one can make you change, you have to choose to change
  • You can never make anyone else change, they have to choose to change
  • You can not buy happiness. but you can absolutely buy away unhappiness.
  • Not every problem has to be fixed. Not every disagreement has to be resolved. Not every insecurity has to be conquered. Choose your battles wisely
  • If you are not growing or changing, you are not living. Many people in this world are alive. but not living.

如何每天活上24小时

听播客提到一本书：如何每天活上24小时，是一本1908年出版的效率书籍。播客主分享了书的大意与精华。他说：作者Arnold Bennett观察到伦敦白领朝九晚五生活的普遍问题是，对朝九晚五生活的不满足和忧虑，对此作者给出了一个解决办法。

办法并不是如何榨干你的剩余时间和精力，而是通过一周7小时的思考和行动，找到自己的目标，通过理性的认识，付之于行动，最终过上一个满足，有意义的人生。

最近刚看到一个有趣的说法，他说：有一类好书不必精读，这类书的特点是，书的结论你已经知道，但一直没做到。这类书，知道大意即可，不如把读书的时间剩下来，直接用于执行。

我觉得这本，如何每天活24小时 就是这样一本书。所以听过等于读过。接下来只需实践即可。

故事#3:what really motivates people

A few months after my husband and I moved to a small Massachusetts town I grumbled to a resident about the poor service at the library, hoping she would repeat my complaints to the librarian. The next time I went to the library, the librarian had set aside two bestsellers for me and a new biography for my husband. what is more, she appeared to be genuinely glad to see me.

Later I reported the miraculous change to my friend. “I suppose you told her how poor we thought the service was?” I asked.

“No,” She confessed. “In fact-I hope you do not mind - I told her your husband was amazed at the way she had built up this small town library, and that you thought she showed unusually good taste in the new books she ordered”

数字断舍离

Newsletter事不过三的最新一期讲到数字断舍离，作者说他限制自己每天的阅读数量，最多不超过十条，同时给自己定了一个写作任务，每天更新三条，不论长短，并取名为十三邀。

因为有限制，所以对点开的内容就会有所挑选，会多问自己：为什么想点开它，期待从中获得什么，真的愿意把时间交给它吗

决定打开它之后，展开一场深度对话。如果是短文，浏览完后，迅速整理。如果是长篇，打印出来，边读边记，如果是视频，放到飞书妙计上导出文字版，然后整理，如果是书，尽量在篇章未直接写笔记。

作者还给自己搭建了一个*写作系统，每天在上面更新，篇幅无论长短，每天最少一条，最多三条。

有了阅读和习作系统后，就会明白，写作并不依赖灵感，而是以来系统和习惯。
