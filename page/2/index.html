David Perell 50天写作课程（六）:记笔记是一场时空的旅行 Note-Taking is Time Travel

翻看照片会唤起我们过往的各种被遗忘的记忆。一张布拉格的美食照片，会让你想起在那里吃过的最美味的鱼，住过的最charing的airbnb旅馆，还有和朋友一起走过的查理大桥。是的, Photos do not just remind you of what you captured. They elicit memories of the other places you visited, the people you were with, and the emotions you felt while traveling.

记笔记也是如此。It is about having conversations with your past and furture self so you can develop ideas over time. 格莱美奖得主Kendrick Lamar曾说过：Note-taking is the closest thing we have to time traving.

David Perell说：I have to make notes because a lot of my inspiration comes from meeting people or going outside the country or going around the corner of my old neighborhood and talking to a five-year-old little boy. and I have to write them down and then five or 3 months later, I have to find that same emotion that I felt when I was inspired by it, so I have to dig deep to see what triggered the idea.. It comes back because I have key little words that make me realize the exact emotion which drew the inspiration.

Words on the page have infinite patience. by taking notes, Kendrick travels through time and rebels against the entropy of memory.

David Perell 50天写作课程（五）：写作让你成为更好的演讲者 Writing makes you a better speaker

Neil DeGrasse Tyson在一次接受David Perell的访谈中曾经说，他会把要在公共场合说的话全部提前写下来。通过这样频繁的写作训练，锻炼了他公共演讲的节奏感，也成为美国最雄辩的科学家。

Writing sharpens the substance of his ideas. Unlike speech, works on page can be reworked and rewritten until they are clear and concise. by the time he sits down for an interview, the hard work of thinking is complete and he turns into a carrier for ideas he has already penned.

If you want to become a better speaker, start by writing every day.

David Perell 50天写作课程（四）：写作不只是打字

David Perell 50天写作课程（四）：写作不只是打字 Writing is More than Typing

David Perell 的比喻总是很妙。

今天他从最爱的一部纪录片 Jiro Dreams of Sushi 讲起，提到法式餐饮中的一个词：Mise-en-place, 即一切就位，是说厨师在真正开始烹饪之前，有至少数小时的准备工作，比如纪录片里真正cooking的部分是极少的，每日去鱼市选鱼的部分是他最爱的场景。

写作和厨师的工作很像。厨师要负责：Creating menus, preparing the food, plant design, managing kitchen staff, ordering equipment and controlling the ambiance. 只有这一切完成之后才开始cooking. Cooking只是厨师众多活动中的一小部分。同样，the online writing experience extends to sourcing ideas, taking notes, having conversations, managing emotional turmoil, putting words on the page, distributing essays and building audience. Typing is only a fraction of what it means to be a writer.

David Perell 50天写作课程（三）：打造自己的笔记系统

要想提高写作，从做一个好的note-taker开始。

note-taking system should not just help you save ideas, it should help you generate new ones too. 就如同你去超市，不总是只买回购物清单上的物件，而是也会带回了一些计划之外的东西。

如何成为一个好的note-taker:

  1. Save only the best notes.

    Do not hoard information, save your top 5-10% of ideas only. that way, you can trust that everything in your note-taking system is high quality.

  2. Create evergreen notes

    Like a good investment, the benefits of your note-taking system should compound in value. save ideas that will stay relevant for many years. Read the classics, skip the news. Note-by-note, brick-by-brick you will build an intellectual Coliseum.

  3. Publish regularly

  4. Be lazy

    Your note-taking system doe not have to be perfect. there is only one way to measure the value of a not-taking system: how much does it help you produce?

  5. Research happens in the background

    Compile notes over time, once you have writing immediately-right when you have an epiphany. Start researching after you have written a draft.

  6. Link notes together

    Organize your idea by topic, not by source. As you browse your not-taking system, consider the serendipity you want to create for your future self. For example, if you read two books about a topic, link those notes together.

  7. Note-taking is a form of time-travel.

  8. The ultimate goal of not-taking is to improve your ideas.

    the goad of note-taking is not to save information. it is to have ideas you would not have hat otherwise. To be smarter, faster, and more creative.

  9. Practice instant outlines.

  10. Keep things messy.

    Ditch perfection, embrace randomness.

接下来一段时间的研究议题将会是：如何构建笔记系统，用什么工具构建。边研究边实践，打造出自己的笔记系统雏形。

David Perell 50天写作课程（二）：为什么写作 why you should write

文字是互联网的原子单位。

只要动动笔，你就可以建立人脉，提升思维并创造机会。

Derek Silver曾说：The coolest people I meet are the ones who find me through something I have written.

A well-written article can change your life because the internet rewards people who think well. Each post is an advertisement for the kinds of people and opportunities you want to attract and if you have a voice, you can build a platform.

In any field, the most successful people double as writer. Chefs write recipes, comedians write jokes, and entrepreneurs write business plans. The example are endless.

Jeff Bezos said: People write a lot, also listen a lot . they also change their mind a lot . Not necessarily with new data, but sometimes re-analyzing the same data. They also work hard to disconfirm fundamental biases.

An empty white page is a mirror into your mind. when the ideas in your minds are clouded, so are the words on the page in front of you. Re-writing is re-thinking. It is the single best way to sharpen your ideas.

when you know you are going to write, you change the way you live. You can no longer sleepwalk through life. The most powerful insigts come from everyday experiences that people ignore.

Sherlock Holmes said: The world is full of obvious things which nobody by any chance ever observes.

Writing initiates the ultimate positive feedback loop. Online writer are rewarded with instant feedback, and fast feedback loops are the best way to accelerate your learning. better yet, writing regularly will inspire you to live an interesting life. As your writing improves, so will the opportunaties available to you.

James Clear says: if you wish you would take something more seriously, do it publicly.. social pressure forces you to up your game.

Writing is free. The only thing standing between you and your writing goals is discipline. so learn to write.

David Perell 50天写作课程（一）：为自己而写

订阅了David Perell的50天写作课程，一天一封关于写作技巧的指导邮件，昨天收到第一封，记录一下。

第一天：为自己而写（Write for yourself）

主播Tyler Cowen在他的每期播客中都这样开场：This is the conversation I want to have, not the one you want to have. 听众如果感到失望，他不会在意。因为He wants to have wild and wacky conversations that reflect his interests. by ignoring the wisdom of crowds, he attracted an ocean of like-minded super fans.

Depending on the article, you can either write for yourself or one other person. Thinking about thousands of people at once will drive you crazy because you will not know what to focus on. Do not let a stadium of perspectives paralyze you. Pick one obsessive person to write for.

简言之：为自己而写，写自己感兴趣的话题，最终自然会吸引到志同道合的读者。

断更一次会怎样

日更这个blog超一个月了，一个问题自然就冒出来了：断更一次会怎样? 为此还去搜索了一番，最后在James Clear那里找到了满意的答案。

Every habit streak ends at some point. Perfection is not possible. Before long, an emergency will pop up - you get sick or you have to travel for work or your family needs a little of your time. Whatever this happens to me, I try to remind myself of a simple rule:

Never miss twice.

Generally speaking, the first mistake is never the one that ruins you. It is the spiral of repeated mistakes that follows.

Missing once is an accident, Missing twice is the start of a new habit.

You do not realize how valuable it is to just show up on your bad (or busy) days. Lost days hurt you more than successful days help you.

Noah Kagan的故事

看到推特上有人提到Noah Kagan这个人，thread了8点他的行事风格对他的巨大影响。

  1. Prioritize NOW, not how

    他说：Noah’s instince is to act first, then think later based on feedback. 他则相反：He wanted to over-research stuff first. 但显然：actually doing the thing first gets real-time feedback which is way better than research.

  2. Test then invest

  3. Have ONE clear goal

    Noah learned this from Zucks during his Facebook days. This makes decision-making Way easier.

  4. Default to NO

    with main goal in mind, say NO to things that did not help to get there.

    He said he realized that the real magic of producitivity is to just focus on the few critical drivers and ignore everything else.

  5. What is working & how can we do more of it

  6. Feedback is gift.

  7. Face discomfort

  8. Make it fun.

好奇之下Google了一下Noah Kagan，原来他是Facebook特别早期的员工（#30号），05年加入facebook，当年24岁, 9个月后却被公司开除。为此他损失了1，85亿美元的FB股票，于是他写了一本书，名叫 How I lost 170 Million Dollars: My Time as #30 at Facebook. 书中解释了他被解雇的原因以及他如何在Raddit的”Ask my anything”的聊天中重新成为一名成功企业家的。

尽管他错过了ＦＢ的财富，但此后他创立了自己的２家成功的公司，Sumo和AppSumo, 每家也都有上Million的价值。此外他还主持一个名为”Noah Kagan Presents”的商业播客和拥有一个YouTube频道。可谓是：what does not kill us makes us stronger.

关于如何度过艰难时光，他说：What helped was keeping busy , surrounding myself with positive people and reflecting on what I can learn from the situation.

他对企业家的建议是：Persist. Things take time so do not give up for a month, Impressive things take years. 谈到创业的最佳时机，他建议创业者最好立即开始：Money is everywhere. Go make something so people are EXCITED to give it to you.
