Choose Carefully

畅销书作家Seth Godin有个做法深深触动到我，他说What you track determins your lens,所以要choose carefully.

有些人keeping tracking of how many times he has been rejected. Keeping track of how many times it did not wor. Keeping tracking of all the times someone has broken his heart or double crossed him or let him down. 当然我们可以keep tracking of those things, but why? why keep track of them? Are they making us better?

Wouldn’t id make more sense to keep track of the other stuff? To keep track of all the times it worked? All the times we took a risk? All the times we were able to brighten someone else’s day? When we start doing that, we can redefine ourselves as people who are able to make an impact on the world. It took me a bunch of cycles to figure out that the narrative was up to me.

Good

美国海军指挥官Jacko Willink说他有一个相当简单的方法来处理挫败，失败，延误或其它灾难，那就是说”Good”(好). 因为when things are going bad, there is going to be good that will come from it.

比如：

Did not get promoted? Good, More time to get better

Did not get the job you want? Good, Go out, gain more experience, and build a better Resume

Unexpcted problem? Good. We have the opportunity to figure out a solution.

When things are going bad, do not get all bummed out, do not get startled. Just accept reality, but focus on the solution. Take that issue, take that setback, take that problem and turn it into something good. Go forward.

If you can say the word good, it means that you are still alive, it means that you are still breathing.

So get up, dust off, reload, recalibrate, re-engage and go out on the attack.

Derek Sivers式专注

Derek Sivers真的是一个特立独行的人，当成功人士都在大谈特谈他们的良好习惯，比如Morning Routings的时候。 Derek Sivers说除了吃饭，写作，它完全没有每天早上都做的那种morning routings. 他给出的原因是：I get really, really, really into one thing at a time, 比如一年前他想优化他的PostgreSQL数据库的时候，他花了5个月的时间去做这件事，在他醒着的所有时间，他完全沉浸在这一件事情上(I spent 5 months- every waking hour-just completely immersed in this one thing). 真是一位超级专注的人。

然后他说：Then after 5 months, I finished that project. I took a week and I went hiking in Milford Sound in New Zealand. Totally offline. When I got back from that, I was so zennature-boy that I spend the next couple of weeks just reading books outside.

  • Desire is suffering (Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want)

Rules for a better life

Rules for a better life：

  1. Wake up early

  2. Focus on effort, not results

  3. Read every day

  4. Strict with yourself, tolerant with others

  5. You control what you do, you control the standards you hold yourself to.

  6. Seek out challenges

  7. Be comfortable being uncomfortable

  8. Stay a student. focus on what is left to learn, not what you have already discovered.

索引式学习法

索引式学习法（geekplux.com）

要义是指在学习一项技能时，先不深究其细节，而是建立索引。就像看书时先看目录一样，把目录多看几遍，对整本书的结构有所了解之后，在去看自己想看的章节。索引学习即：我们只需要知道有这个东西，在脑中建立这部分知识的索引，等用到他的时候再去查阅资料重新学习就好。

所以，索引学习法的核心点：把精力专注再当前节点，其次，强调用时才学，学完之后马上运用，实践会加深对知识的理解。

建立索引的核心方法：要了解该技能每一处知识点或功能的用处是什么，它能解决什么问题，遇到什么问题时需要用到这块知识。

如何有意识地活着

正在读一本书：DO EPIC SHIT，挑了”意识”一章先看起来，收获如下:

  1. Doing the things that are the most important are the things that make you important.

  2. Being calm is a skill (Being calm when you are ridiculed/left alone/questioned)

  3. Satisfaction is the most powerful skill to have(Satisfaction in your journey/how far you have come/all the unwanted parts of you you have scooped out)

  4. Awareness is the start of the decision, not the end of it.

    You know. You act. You learn. You know better. And you continue.

  5. Comparing yourself to others is the biggest waste of time.

  6. Think of the time that lies ahead, not of the time that lies in the past

  7. People will help you only when you have helped them understand how they can.

  8. The distance between “ I could have” and “ I have” is regret.

  9. The real problem was never the real problem. The real problem was the compound interest of complaining.

  10. You will get what you seek, not what you desire, not what you dream.
